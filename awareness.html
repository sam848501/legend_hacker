AIDS Awareness

Overview

Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS) is a chronic condition caused by the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV). It severely damages the immune system, making the body vulnerable to infections and diseases.

Symptoms

Symptoms of HIV/AIDS can vary, but common signs include:

  • Flu-like symptoms (fever, fatigue, swollen lymph nodes)
  • Rapid weight loss
  • Skin rashes or bumps
  • Night sweats
  • Persistent diarrhea
  • Opportunistic infections

Prevention

Preventing HIV transmission is crucial. Here are some effective methods:

  • Use condoms during sexual intercourse.
  • Consider pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) if at high risk.
  • Avoid sharing needles or syringes.
  • Get tested regularly for HIV.

Treatment

While there is no cure for HIV/AIDS, effective treatment options are available:

  • Antiretroviral therapy (ART) can help manage the virus.
  • Regular medical check-ups to monitor health.
  • Support groups and counseling for emotional well-being.
